## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

7 Trends for B2B Marketing in 2025 that should happen but probably won’t:

1. Smaller Internal W2 Fixed Cost Marketing Teams 

We will see more 10-person Marketing teams at $100MM ARR than we will 100-person teams that many are accustomed to. Tons of management layers, reporting workflows, and inflated overhead costs that actually slow you down.

2. Senior leaders will get back into Execution.

No more sitting in Ivory towers with 4 management layers between Strategy and Execution. Smaller teams means senior leaders are in the execution, close to customers, and learn how to use AI to exponentially increase their productivity.

3. Greater usage of Offshoring & AI (more flexible, less costly)

Low level executional roles like video editing, graphic design, (potentially) paid media execution, email prospecting, etc. will be executed by AI and operated/managed by 1 expert in the craft.

4. Greater usage of External experts that bring process, data & automation that augments internal staff & builds competitive advantages.

For almost a decade, B2B companies have taken pride in trying to do everything in-house (see Point 1 above). And they miss out on amazing process, data, automation, insights, and expertise from external experts that simply cannot be replicated in-house.

5. Greater investment and weighted budgeting to program dollars and less to W2 headcount.

The typical budget allocation is 55/45 Programs to Headcount investment in the Marketing budget. Over time, we will see that more weighted to 75/25 Programs to Headcount as companies get more efficient on Headcount strategy, implement AI and offshoring. 

This adjustment creates meaningfully more flexibility for the Revenue Factory.

6. CMOs that don’t own the Prospecting machine (e.g. BDRs) are at a severe disadvantage

It’s true. Process efficiency & alignment matter. If your BDRs & Marketing team spend most of the time debating “Inbound” vs. “Outbound”, you are almost guaranteed to miss your targets.

7. CMOs that aren’t intently focused on unit economics & ROI will be fired by Mid-2026

Boards are about to put the hammer down on Marketing. If they haven't already. CMOs that communicate a clear, simple, financially-driven story to the board around performance, opportunities and future plans will succeed. Those that continue to present “marketing sourced revenue reports” , “number of MQLs or demo requests”, “influenced revenue reports” and over-complicated, unhelpful touchpoint data won’t have their job in 12 months.

PE investments that invested 2021-2022 have 2-3 more years left on the hold and they aren’t happy with the current return profile. Growth needs to accelerate and CAC needs to go down big time to get the exit multiple they want.

__

Everything changed in 2022-2023. Most of the traditional Marketing playbook for a CMO is now set up to get you fired, not rewarded. 

It's a new era for CMOs. Are you stuck in the past or paving the way forward?
