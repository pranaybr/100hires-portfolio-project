## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

Most of the “problems” people debate on LinkedIn aren’t the real problem.

-Executive churn (CMO/CRO tenure: 18 months)
-Constant copying of products and messaging
-No “storytelling”
-The recycled, boring “brand vs demand” debate

These are surface-level symptoms. They’re visible and easy to point to. 

The real problem? A low-frequency company. 
It’s invisible, fundamental, and systemic. 

In low frequencies, scarcity, competition, and urgency drive behavior. 

-Copying competitors becomes safer than originality
-Fear of being wrong outweighs vision. 
-Metrics are worshipped over mission & impact
-“Best practices” conformity trumps original thinking 
-Employees and leaders are rewarded for playing it safe instead of creating something bold. 
-Everyone feels drained, replaceable, “not enough”, falling behind, and stuck 

This invisible energy drives how people feel, the decisions they make, and what gets rewarded. You can feel it. It's heavy and constricting.

This frequency gets written into the foundation.

It’s highly contagious and spreads rapidly. Soon everyone in the company thinks, feels, and acts from that energy. 

—

The solution isn't better "storytelling" or a team offsite. 

It's raising the Frequency of the company. 

Vision > Competition
Abundance > Scarcity
Resonance > Urgency
Originality > Conformity

In practice:

-Teams feel energized, not drained

-Leaders raise their frequency through self-leadership, empowering the company with clarity, abundance, and purpose. 

-Companies communicate bold truth, magnetizing their people and repelling misaligned teammates, customers, and partners. 

-Success is measured by impact, integrity, and sustainability

__

It sucks to be a teammate, customer, partner, or vendor of a low-frequency company. 

As people tune into this, the dynamics of business will change for the better, forever. 

The future belongs to high-frequency companies.

#frequency #business #leadership

p.s. As a high-value contributor and creator, when you are evaluating the next company to join, the most important thing you can do for your life, family, career, and well-being is avoid low-frequency companies at all costs.
