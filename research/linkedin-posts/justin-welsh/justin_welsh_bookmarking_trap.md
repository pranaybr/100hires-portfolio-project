## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

Stop bookmarking content.

It's killing your ability to actually get better.

The problem is that content is designed to get you to save, read, watch, or listen.

It's not generally designed to make you better at something specific.

I see this happen to so many people. 
Myself included.

It goes a little something like this:

1. You want to learn a skill
2. You go to YouTube.
3. You save a "comprehensive tutorial."
4. You find another one. You save it.
5. And another one and another and another.

Two months later, you've got 25+ videos ready to watch, and you're still waiting for the right time to get started.

I recently did this when trying to learn how to use Claude Code.

I made absolutely zero progress.

Then one morning, about two months ago, I trashed all of those videos and asked one simple six-word question that jump-started my entire journey.

Now? I feel like an absolute pro.

There's a specific reason this happened, and once I understood it, I couldn't stop thinking about it.

I'm sharing it in tomorrow's Saturday Essay. 

If you're an aspiring entrepreneur asking yourself, "How do I get started?"...
