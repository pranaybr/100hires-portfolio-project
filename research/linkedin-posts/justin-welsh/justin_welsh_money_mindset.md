## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

Most people think about money backward.

And it wrecks them.

They spend their whole lives trying to afford a nicer version of what they already have. 

A better car, a bigger house, and the next fancy upgrade. 

The stuff other people can see. 

And it feels like progress because it *looks* like progress.

I played that game pretty damn hard in my thirties. 

Good title.
Good salary.
Two luxury cars. 
The whole image. 

I thought that was what "winning" looked like.

It took one financial disaster to show me I had it completely upside down. 

The people I now consider wealthy aren't playing that game at all. 

They're building something most people never even think about, nobody can see, and nobody will ever compliment them on. 

But it's the only thing that actually matters when life throws the unexpected curveball at them.

Watching them succeed changed how my wife and I handle every dollar we make. 

It's probably the most important money lesson I've learned in 25 years of earning it.

I wrote about it this week.

If you've ever felt like you're making money and still somehow anxious about it, this one's for you.

Read it below.
