## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

I've built an audience of 300K+ across LinkedIn, X, and email.
My advantage? I had a few, actually.

Most people growing fast followings had an edge of some sort. No shade. We all have one or two. The winners just spot theirs early and lean in.

5 Audience Growth Advantages

// Proof Advantage
When you've already done the thing at a high level, you don't have to fight for your credibility. Your track record does it for you.

Codie Sanchez spent 15 years on Wall Street and owned a stack of laundromats and car washes before posted her first video. When she talks about buying boring businesses, she’s got receipts.

// Beauty Advantage
Uncomfortable? Yes. But the research is blunt: we pay more attention and trust attractive faces more. The halo effect is real.

Sahil Bloom is creates banging content. He’s also an ex-athlete who could pass for a model—that helps. First, he built a huge audience. Now he's leaning into his biological advantage with his new men's skincare line. Smart.

// Time Advantage
The more time you have to create content, comment, and hone your craft, the faster you grow. Some people simply have more hours in the day than others.

Lara Acosta admits that she started building her audience from her dorm room, grinding 4–5 hours a day just commenting. Having more free time is a real edge. (And near impossible to copy when you've got a business to run and a family.)

*Timing* is the other half of it. People like me who got on platforms early—before the AI slop-fest when growth was easier—got luckier than later entrants.

// Money Advantage
Then there's an enviable one: you’re rich. Money allows you to hire an all-star content team. You still need to bring killer ideas and charisma, but having a team is leverage.

When Alex Hormozi launched Acquisition, he'd already built and sold his first group of companies. Rumor is he had a $50k/month content team within the first 6 months of building his personal brand. Big investment. Big results.

These are 4 real advantages, right?

And if you've got one, use it. The people winning use theirs.

The 5th advantage is a bit different...

// Insight Advantage
Do you approach your work differently? Are you willing to think in public—even if it means people will scrutinize and disagree with you? Having fresh insights *and* guts to show your raw thinking is a real advantage.

Smart folks like Amanda Natividad, Alex M H Smith, and Rob and Anthony from FletchPMM are great examples. They share their best thinking publicly. Even when it invites backlash or inspires copycats.

And I do the same. I’ve made millions from my online courses, and every framework I teach was sharpened by posting about it first. 

Luckily for big-brained experts like you, the Insight Advantage is the one nobody's born with and nobody can buy. You just need the guts to think in public.

So what’s holding you back?
