## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

Video *is* Zero Click Marketing. Most B2B teams aren't treating it that way.

To do Zero Click Marketing means to deliver value in the feed. No click required. But most B2B video is designed backwards.

A teaser. Or, the full insight... without a clear next step for where to go or what to do next.

The teams getting real traction are treating video as the destination, not a means to an end.

• The full idea lives in the video itself (with a natural next step if someone wants more)
• Distribution gets built around the video, not bolted on after
• Repurposing is the strategy with clear additional use cases

Video designed to be discovered is a different beast from video designed to be promoted.
It's why my video output looks different in different places. A SparkToro Office Hours webinar is built for our customers. A Zero Click Marketing podcast episode is built for YouTube discovery. Similar audience (usually), different jobs to be done.

I have a feeling that's where a lot of teams get stuck. They say, “We need more video,” when they should be asking themselves, "What is this video supposed to accomplish *without* requiring someone to leave the place they found it?"
