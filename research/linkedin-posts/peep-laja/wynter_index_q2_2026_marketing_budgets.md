## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

B2B marketing budgets keep climbing. Performance doesn't.

Wynter Index Q2 2026 dropped today. 200 B2B SaaS marketing leaders surveyed.

Ad spend is the standout: roughly four times as many teams are raising paid budgets as cutting them. The fastest accelerator in the index, and still climbing.

Software spend, headcount, and agency spend all expanded too, but mildly. The buying spree on tools that defined Q1 has moderated.

Then there's performance to plan. Dead even. As many teams missed their number this quarter as hit it.

Spend is up across every lever. Hitting plan is a coin flip.

Three things explain it:

1. AI search is rerouting the funnel.

31% of marketers named AEO, GEO, or AI search erosion as a Q2 priority. A year ago this was a rounding error.

One Enterprise marketer at a former 90% SEO-driven shop: they've seen sharp drops in SEO traffic and revenue, and are pivoting their team toward holistic earned media plus AEO.

Another: "GEO pilots are delivering less traffic, but more highly engaged and ready to purchase users."

Top of funnel has moved to LLMs (and Gartner predicts LLMs will produce 95% in the future). 

2. LinkedIn is the most polarizing channel in B2B.

38% of marketers are betting on LinkedIn this quarter. More than any other channel.

7% are explicitly cutting it. The most-named cut for ROI reasons.

Same channel. Top of the bet list AND the cut list.

A VP of Demand Gen: "CPL has increased significantly and the quality has been declining for some time. We are not seeing ROI."

LinkedIn is becoming a "you crack it or you bail" channel. There's no middle.

3. The paid lever is the shortest path to motion.

Marketers are under pressure to produce results, fast. Paid ads seems to be the fastest way to get there. 

However marketers seem to be pulling the lever that moves fastest, not necessarily the one that moves furthest. Performance hasn't moved for most.
