## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

"I don't know how to grow on LinkedIn anymore"

Start at level 1 of the 2026 LinkedIn game:

LinkedIn rebuilt its ranking system this year. The old tricks quietly stopped working.

Treat the rebuild as a game, and leveling up gets weirdly addictive.

Level 1: Old playbook

Posting at 8am sharp won't save a weak post
Leave your pod, it filters fake AI comments now
Dwell time counts, not first-hour velocity
Quit hashtags, the AI reads meaning, not labels

Level 2: The 360 shift

"360Brew" replaced LinkedIn's old ranking models
Rewrite your headline in your buyer's words
Choose the right type of industry in your intro
Your company and title fields ride every post

Level 3: First 50 words = audition

Count the first 50 words of your last 5 posts
Put your topic and your buyer in the opening line
The AI decides early if you make the pool
Write a hook for humans and the model at once

Level 4: Dwell time over likes

Go one level deeper than everyone else
Write things others would stop, read, share and forward.
Reply to every comment in the first two hours
Time spent reading counts as much as a like.

Level 5: Topic depth over breadth

Pick one lane and commit to it for 90 days
Drop the generic "5 tips" posts, they die now
Go deep enough the AI knows who to show you
Make your last 20 posts read as one topic

Level 6: Follower bias gone

Stop blaming your follower count. Start posting.
A strong 5K profile's post beats a median 25K one
Use the structural edge small accounts have right now
Let execution beat audience size

Level 7: AI search visibility

Structure posts so an LLM can quote you to your buyer
Answer the exact question your ICP types into AI tools
Write content that's specific and easy to cite
Show up where the model already sources its answers

Level 8: "How I" over "how to"

Swap "how to" framing for "how I" wherever you can
Tell the real story behind your client wins
Share the messy version AI can't invent
Anchor every lesson to something real

Level 9: Topic domination

One engine feeding LinkedIn, YouTube, and email
Repeat your themes until they tie to your name
Become the name dropped when your topic comes up
Stop competing post by post and own the category

Finished all nine?

LinkedIn keeps rebuilding.

Keep leveling up or get left in the old playbook.
