## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

Nobody wants to do what it actually takes.
And I have bad news, because it takes...a L-O-T.

Google finally confirmed that optimizing for AI is, basically, still SEO. 
Its generative features pull from the same ranking and quality systems as traditional search. 

💔 So friend, if you can't rank, you likely won't get cited.💔 

Then there's the question of where AI actually goes to read. 

Semrush data shows the most-cited domains are Reddit, LinkedIn, Wikipedia and YouTube. Not brand websites. AI reads the internet and repeats what it finds.

The logic 👇: 
AI won't mention you if the internet isn't already talking about you. 
It won't cite you if you don't bring anything new. Academic research found that original data, stats, and expert quotes can lift AI visibility by up to 40%. 

👂 You heard that correctly: generating more slop won't help you.

And finally, dear CEOs, ranking 1st in AI might not be the godsend you think it is. Another study by Semrush surveyed 1,000 consumers [ 👉 check it out: https://bit.ly/3Rxlo3g] and found that being first in an AI answer makes a brand stand out for only 20% of them. 

The rest notice a brand because of how clearly and relevantly it's described, not because it showed up first.

So stop chasing hacks to rank "number one on ChatGPT."
Build the brand the internet wants to talk about.
