## ── THE RAW SWIPE TEXT ──

In 2026, ranking on Google means having a solid social media strategy.

A few years ago, that would have sounded BANANAS. 🍌 

Now it's in front of your eyes. 

AI search engines build answers by pulling mainly from third-party sources, and traditional organic search has shifted right along with it.

Case in point: googling "How do you create motion graphics for LinkedIn?"

🔍 The AI Overview cites LinkedIn, YouTube, and a couple of blogs
 🔍 The video section pulls from YouTube
 🔍 The discussion section pulls from Reddit threads
 🔍 And even the "traditional" organic results on page one are all pulling from LinkedIn (!!!)

The rationale checks out: ask a question about LinkedIn, and the engine assumes LinkedIn is the most authoritative source to answer it.

What's worth a second look is which LinkedIn content made the cut.

Among the LinkedIn results, exactly ONE was a post, and it had FOUR likes. Everything else was a LinkedIn Learning page or an article.

Here's what's actually going on:

1/ LinkedIn's citation share is climbing fast across the board
Profound tracked LinkedIn moving from 11th to 5th most-cited domain on ChatGPT between November and February. Semrush puts it at 14.3% of ChatGPT Search citations, behind only Reddit.

2/ The format that gets cited isn't the format that gets engagement
50 to 66% of LinkedIn's AI citations come from articles, not posts. The metric you've been optimizing for and the metric AI engines reward are two different things.

3/ Video is becoming major citation real estate
YouTube citations in Google AI Overviews grew 34% in six months, according to NP Digital.

If you've been running content channel by channel, chasing likes on one platform and views on another, this is your sign to start thinking in terms of consistent messaging, original content, and strong distribution loops.

Thanks to AI, execution has never been easier.
Finding something worth saying, on the other hand, has gotten harder.
