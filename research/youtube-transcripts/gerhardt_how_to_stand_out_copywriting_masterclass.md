## TL;DR
The proliferation of generative AI tools has created a "velocity trap" in corporate marketing, leading to an environment saturated with homogenized, predictable content. Eddie Schlainer (Founder of Very Good Copy) makes the operational case that **copywriting remains a uniquely human profession** because persuasive writing relies on psychological empathy, shared vulnerability, and narrative tension rather than linguistic perfection. While AI excels at the structural automation of secondary activities like data curation and copy research, it is fundamentally incapable of generating inspired messaging. To stand out against modern automated noise, B2B growth operators must intentionally apply creative friction, utilizing advanced narrative techniques such as dialogue framing, anti-description, and subtext to build deep human connections that cross-platform algorithms cannot replicate.

---

## The AI Narrative Shift and the Core Quality Crisis

B2B audiences and marketing professionals are showing a clear, measurable pushback against algorithmic content output. The baseline market sentiment has shifted away from praising AI efficiency toward a distinct fatigue with automated text production.

This backlash highlights a fundamental technical limitation in how Large Language Models (LLMs) process human experience:
* **The Sterilization of Language:** AI models are engineered to predict the most probable, statistically average sequence of words. This structural design results in an uninspired, predictable voice that strips out the creative anomalies required for memorable writing.
* **The Spoon-Feeding Problem:** Automated writing frequently over-explains situations, directly instructing the reader on how to feel about a given moment rather than creating space for individual interpretation.
* **The Reality Matrix:** AI tools lack personal history, somatic memory, and genuine real-world perspective. Consequently, they default to idealized, cliché summaries of human milestones rather than capturing the nuanced friction of lived experiences.

## The Inspired Writing Blueprint: Crafting "Holes" in Narrative

To break through the modern filter of user indifference, copy must move beyond clear syntax and introduce structural "holes" that allow the target audience to actively participate in the text. This narrative technique leverages the **Kuleshov Effect**—a film theory demonstrating that audiences naturally project their own deep context, memory, and emotions onto neutral frames or minimalist text.

### 1. Dialogue Framing
* **Operational Mechanics:** Swapping descriptive, third-person corporate summaries for active, raw dialogue. 
* **Strategic Value:** Real dialogue forces the reader to instantly judge character intentions and evaluate high-pressure situations based on what is explicitly said versus what is withheld.

### 2. Anti-Description
* **Operational Mechanics:** Intentionally omitting broad, generalized environmental descriptions in favor of a few hyper-specific, evocative sensory anchors.
* **Strategic Value:** Instead of defining a whole setting (e.g., explicitly stating, *"We were in a sterile corporate office"*), the text calls out minimalist details like masks, gowns, and gloves. This restraint prompts the user's brain to fill in the rest of the scene using their own memory, creating an instantly believable mental image.

### 3. Structural Subtext
* **Operational Mechanics:** Layering the layout so the underlying significance of a message is dictated by the structural context of the scene rather than direct explanations.
* **Strategic Value:** Allowing core emotional shifts and transformations to happen quietly off the page. This subtle approach invites the reader to discover the true point of the copy on their own, resulting in a much deeper psychological impact.

## The Strategic Path Forward for B2B Growth Operators

Copywriting and demand generation are ultimately exercises in radical empathy—the disciplined practice of temporarily adopting the specific worldview, fears, and internal incentives of a core market segment.

| Growth Metric | Core Channel Defensive Strategy | Operational Implementation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Defeating Filter Blindness** | Modern buyers are subconsciously filtering out perfectly structured, corporate AI template copy. | Replace template outreach with highly human, asymmetrical layout styles. |
| **The Handmade Advantage** | High-converting creative relies on unproduced, authentic execution over polished agency assets. | Deploy raw field updates, video clips, and text insights posted directly from mobile devices. |
| **Empirical Message Testing** | Treat organic distributions as low-stakes live testing grounds before scaling media assets. | Use personal social feeds to validate messaging resonance before allocating paid performance ad budgets behind them. |

The future bar for organic differentiation has been elevated. True competitive advantage belongs to the growth teams that combine the lightning-fast data aggregation of AI with the unfiltered, creative friction of real human perspectives.
